Abstract This systematic literature review (SLR) explores the applications of data mining and machine learning (ML) across various real-world domains. The review follows a structured methodology, analyzing peer-reviewed literature from 2015 to 2024. Key findings indicate a growing adoption of ML techniques in healthcare, finance, marketing, and environmental science. The study identifies dominant trends, emerging challenges, and knowledge gaps. 1. Introduction Data mining and machine learning have transformed decision-making across industries. Organizations leverage these technologies to analyze large datasets, uncover patterns, and make predictive models. This review synthesizes research on their applications, identifies trends, and highlights research gaps.

2. Methodology

2.1 Research Questions This review seeks to answer the following questions:

  • What are the primary applications of data mining and ML in real-world domains?
  • What techniques are most frequently used?
  • What challenges and limitations exist in these applications?

2.2 Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria

  • Included: Peer-reviewed journal articles and conference papers (2015-2024) focusing on ML and data mining applications.
  • Excluded: Non-peer-reviewed sources, opinion articles, and papers without empirical evidence.

2.3 Data Sources and Search Strategy Searches were conducted in databases such as IEEE Xplore, Scopus, ACM Digital Library, and Google Scholar using keywords: “data mining applications,” “machine learning real-world use cases,” “ML challenges,” and “AI industry impact.”

2.4 Data Extraction and Analysis Selected studies were analyzed for methodologies, findings, and limitations. Trends and gaps were synthesized to identify research opportunities.

3. Key Findings

3.1 Healthcare Applications ML is revolutionizing healthcare by enabling disease prediction, personalized medicine, and drug discovery. Techniques like deep learning and decision trees improve diagnosis accuracy. Challenges include data privacy concerns and model interpretability (Topol, 2019).

3.2 Finance and Fraud Detection Financial institutions use ML for risk assessment, credit scoring, and fraud detection. Anomaly detection and neural networks help identify suspicious transactions. However, adversarial attacks on ML models pose security risks (Goodfellow et al., 2018).

3.3 Marketing and Consumer Insights E-commerce platforms use ML for recommendation systems and customer sentiment analysis. Clustering and NLP (Natural Language Processing) techniques enhance consumer targeting. However, ethical concerns arise regarding data privacy (Sharma et al., 2021).

3.4 Environmental Science and Sustainability ML assists in climate modeling, pollution detection, and energy optimization. Satellite imagery analysis and reinforcement learning optimize environmental conservation efforts. Data availability and model generalization remain challenges (Gibson et al., 2020).

4. Trends and Emerging Challenges

4.1 Dominant Trends

  • Increasing use of deep learning across domains
  • Rise of automated ML (AutoML) for non-experts
  • Adoption of federated learning to address data privacy concerns

4.2 Key Challenges

  • Bias and fairness issues in ML models
  • Lack of transparency in deep learning algorithms
  • High computational costs and energy consumption

5. Research Gaps and Future Directions

Despite advancements, gaps remain:

  • Limited research on interpretable AI for high-stakes applications
  • Underrepresentation of low-resource regions in data-driven studies
  • Need for standardized evaluation metrics across domains

6. Proposed Hypothesis “The integration of explainable AI techniques into medical diagnosis systems will significantly improve clinician trust and adoption rates compared to traditional black-box ML models.”

7. Conclusion Data mining and ML have made substantial contributions across multiple industries. While their potential is vast, ethical concerns, interpretability, and computational demands present challenges. Future research should focus on making ML models more transparent, fair, and energy-efficient.

Systematic Literature Review: Applications of Data Mining and Machine Learning in Real-World Domains

Abstract This systematic literature review (SLR) explores the applications of data mining and machine learning (ML) across various real-world domains. The review follows a structured methodology, analyzing peer-reviewed literature from 2015 to 2024. Key findings indicate a growing adoption of ML techniques in healthcare, finance, marketing, and environmental science. The study identifies dominant trends, emerging challenges, and knowledge gaps. A testable hypothesis is proposed to guide future research.

1. Introduction Data mining and machine learning have transformed decision-making across industries. Organizations leverage these technologies to analyze large datasets, uncover patterns, and make predictive models. 2. Methodology

2.1 Research Questions This review seeks to answer the following questions: - What are the primary applications of data mining and ML in real-world domains? - What techniques are most frequently used? - What challenges and limitations exist in these applications?

2.2 Inclusion and Exclusion Criteria - Included: Peer-reviewed journal articles and conference papers (2015-2024) focusing on ML and data mining applications. - Excluded: Non-peer-reviewed sources, opinion articles, and papers without empirical evidence.

2.3 Data Sources and Search Strategy Searches were conducted in databases such as IEEE Xplore, Scopus, ACM Digital Library, and Google Scholar using keywords: “data mining applications,” “machine learning real-world use cases,” “ML challenges,” and “AI industry impact.”

2.4 Data Extraction and Analysis Selected studies were analyzed for methodologies, findings, and limitations. Trends and gaps were synthesized to identify research opportunities.

3. Key Findings

3.1 Healthcare Applications ML is revolutionizing healthcare by enabling disease prediction, personalized medicine, and drug discovery. Techniques like deep learning and decision trees improve diagnosis accuracy. Challenges include data privacy concerns and model interpretability (Topol, 2019).

3.2 Finance and Fraud Detection Financial institutions use ML for risk assessment, credit scoring, and fraud detection. Anomaly detection and neural networks help identify suspicious transactions. However, adversarial attacks on ML models pose security risks (Goodfellow et al., 2018).

Satellite imagery analysis and reinforcement learning optimize environmental conservation efforts. Data availability and model generalization remain challenges (Gibson et al., 2020).

4. Trends and Emerging Challenges

4.1 Dominant Trends - Advancements in Deep Learning: Deep learning models, particularly convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and transformers, are becoming increasingly dominant in fields such as image recognition, natural language processing, and autonomous systems (LeCun et al., 2015). - Rise of Automated Machine Learning (AutoML): AutoML frameworks like Google’s AutoML and Microsoft’s Azure ML are making machine learning more accessible to non-experts, reducing the reliance on specialized data scientists (Feurer et al., 2019). - Federated Learning for Privacy-Preserving AI: With growing concerns over data privacy, federated learning enables decentralized training of models without sharing raw data, improving security in healthcare and finance applications (McMahan et al., 2017). - Ethical AI and Explainability: There is an increasing demand for interpretable AI models, especially in high-stakes domains like healthcare and finance, where black-box models are often met with resistance (Doshi-Velez & Kim, 2017).

4.2 Key Challenges - Bias and Fairness in ML Models: Machine learning models can perpetuate biases due to skewed training data, leading to unfair outcomes in hiring, lending, and law enforcement decisions (Buolamwini & Gebru, 2018). - Lack of Transparency in Deep Learning Algorithms: Many deep learning models, particularly neural networks, operate as black boxes, making it difficult for users to understand how decisions are made (Lipton, 2018). - High Computational Costs and Energy Consumption: Training large-scale ML models, such as GPT-4 and BERT, requires massive computational power, raising concerns about sustainability and accessibility (Strubell et al., 2019).

5. Research Gaps and Future Directions

Despite advancements, gaps remain: - Limited Research on Interpretable AI for High-Stakes Applications: More work is needed on explainable AI models that balance performance and transparency, particularly in critical domains like medicine and criminal justice (Molnar, 2020). - Underrepresentation of Low-Resource Regions in Data-Driven Studies: Many ML applications are developed based on datasets from high-income countries, limiting their effectiveness in low-resource environments (He et al., 2021). - Need for Standardized Evaluation Metrics Across Domains: A lack of standardized benchmarks makes it difficult to compare ML model performance across different fields, necessitating better evaluation frameworks (Szegedy et al., 2016).

6. Proposed Hypothesis “The integration of explainable AI techniques into medical diagnosis systems will significantly improve clinician trust and adoption rates compared to traditional black-box ML models.”

7. Conclusion Data mining and ML have made substantial contributions across multiple industries. While their potential is vast, ethical concerns, interpretability, and computational demands present challenges. Future research should focus on making ML models more transparent, fair, and energy-efficient.
